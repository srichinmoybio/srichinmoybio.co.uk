]]> By: Chris Cade http://www.srichinmoybio.co.uk/blog/life/criticism/comment-page-1/#comment-465 Tue, 11 Mar 2008 04:49:22 +0000 http://www.srichinmoybio.co.uk/blog/life/criticism/#comment-465 “For example, point out the mistake but don’t add unnecessary judgments about how bad they are to do it.”

I think that’s the most critical element of all of this. When we honestly remove judgment from our criticisms… they really just become constructive critiques, which is invaluable.

I actually talk a lot about this topic in my upcoming ebook devoted to helping others utilize story writing as a path for spiritual growth.

I also think an important element on this topic is compassion. The more genuine compassion we have for somebody, the more we:

1) Will intuitively know when is the right time to critique and in which ways

2) Will connect with the other person at a deeper level because they’ll know we aren’t saying things to hurt them (although sometimes the truth hurts), but rather we genuinely care for their welfare and just want to help them towards leading a more peaceful, happy, and fulfilling life.
