Literary Achievements of Sri Chinmoy

1944
  • Aged 13 wrote first Poem in English "The Absolute"
1965
  • First issue of Aum Magazine. August 27
  • First lecture in USA: Hicksville High School, Hicksville, NY. October 4
  • First lecture series in the USA began. December 19
1968
  • First lecture at an American university: Yale University. December 4
1969
  • Harvard University talk on Vedanta, 77 years after Vivekananda’s talk. March 25
1970
  • Bhagavad-Gita lectures began at New York University. March 3
  • First book published, My Ivy League Leaves. July 13
  • “The Garland of Nation-Souls,” first lecture at the United Nations, at UN World Youth Conference. July 15
  • First book commercially published, Yoga and the Spiritual Life, by Tower Publications. Fall,
  • Meditations: Food for the Soul published by Harper & Row, NY. Fall
1971
  • Sri Chinmoy’s article “The Vedanta Philosophy” appeared in Journal of the Philosophical Society of England. Spring
  • First Place Poetry Award from Jean’s Journal for Sri Chinmoy’s poem “Between Nothingness and Eternity.” September 1
  • Poem “The Absolute” published in A Yearbook of Modern Poets. Fall
    Songs of the Soul published by Herder & Herder, NY.
  • My Lord’s Secrets Revealed published by Herder & Herder, NY.
1972
  • Upanishad lecture series completed. Begun October 22, 1971. March 1
  • Sri Chinmoy’s article “The Upanishads: India’s Soul-Offering,” appeared in The Princeton Seminary Bulletin. July
  • Completed 50 plays in 8 days. November 23
  • Completed lecture series on the Vedas at the Seven Sister colleges, begun November 14. November 29
  • Arise! Awake! Thoughts of a Yogi published by Frederick Fall, NY.
1973
  • First course on Sri Chinmoy’s philosophy taught for credit, at the University of Connecticut. February 20
  • Kundalini lecture series completed at New York University. March 7
    Began Promised Light of the Beyond lectures at Columbia University, March 28 New York.
  • First wrote 50 poems in one day. May 15
  • UN Secretary-General U Thant attended premier performance of Sri Chinmoy’s Buddha play. New York. May 25
  • Completed The Dance of Life, a 20-volume series of 1,000 poems, June 20 begun on March 23.
  • Wrote 150 poems in 24 hours. October 7
  • Wrote 105 poems in 16 1/2 hours. October 31
  • Wrote 150 poems in 24 hours. November 16
  • Completed The Wings of Light, a 20-volume series of 1,000 poems begun in September. December
  • Commentary on the Bhagavad Gita published by Rudolf Steiner, NY.
  • Mother India’s Lighthouse published by Rudolf Steiner, NY.
  • The Supreme and His Four Children published by Fleet Press Corp., NY.
1974
  • Wrote 208 poems in 22 hours. February 2
  • Wrote 360 poems in 24 hours (The Goal Is Won). April 28
  • Completed The Golden Boat, a 20-volume series of 1,000 poems. October 13 begun on January 29.
  • Completed Fifty Freedom-Boats, a 50-state lecture series, begun January 9. October 29
  • Fifty-one new books published in 10 days under Sri Chinmoy’s direction by the Sri Chinmoy Centre. November 10
  • Completed 200 books. November 10
  • Received the Annual Mentor Poetry Award from the North American Mentor poetry magazine for his poem “0 Bird of Light.”
  • The Inner Promise published by Simon & Schuster, NY.
1975
  • Received a Certificate of Merit from North American Mentor poetry magazine for his poem “The Boat of Time Sails On.” January 23
  • Lecture and meditation at Earth Day Ceremonies, New York City. March 21
  • Finished his seven lecture series at Harvard Divinity School, Cambridge, MA, begun April 16. June 9
  • Wrote 843 poems in 24 hours (Transcendence-Perfection). November 1
  • Sri Chinmoy’s Bicentennial play, The Sacred Fire, performed for the public in Concord, MA, and, in full or in part, throughout 1976 at sites including the Capitol grounds, Washington DC, New York’s Statue of Liberty and the July 4th Rockefeller Center Fair. December
1976
  • Presented his 300 books to New York City Mayor Abraham Beame as a gift to the New York Public Library. December 7
  • 300th book, Aspiration-Tree, published, in the first hardbound edition published by the Sri Chinmoy Centre press. December 17
  • Received the Annual Mentor Poetry Award from the North American Mentor poetry magazine for his poem ‘The Golden Flute.”
  • Poem “O My America” placed in the official archives of the Bicentennial in Washington, DC.
1977
  • 300 books presented to the Free Library of Philadelphia. January 17
  • Thirty-six new books published in two and a half weeks under Sri Chinmoy’s direction by the Sri Chinmoy Centre. April 8
  • Completed Everest-Aspiration, a series of 100 inspiring talks given in 20 days. It was published in a special 4-volume boxed set. Begun July 1. July 20
  • Wrote 630 rhyming poems in 24 hours (A Soulful Cry Versus a Fruitful Smile). August 13
  • Completed From the Source to the Source, a volume of 401 rhyming poems, begun on August 3. August 31
  • Sri Chinmoy’s poetry appeared in Fifty Outstanding American Poets, NY.
1978
  • Presented his 300 books to the Harvard University Library. The books were received by Dr. Peter Oliver, Librarian of the Andover-Harvard Library of the Harvard Divinity School. February 5
  • Completed New York State University lecture/concert series. April 30
    Special publication of Bela Chale Jai, a hardbound book of 47 Bengali poems and translations written in Sri Chinmoy’s own handwriting on the occasion of his 47th birthday. August 27
  • Named Honorary Visiting Scholar, Pacific School of Religion, CA. October 5
1979
  • Completed 100 Indian stories in 19 days. January 24
    Began writing Ten Thousand Flower-Flames, a 100-volume series of 10,000 poems. October 22
  • 400th book, 400 Gratitude-Flower-Hearts, published. A special publication of 92 inspiring messages, most written in his handwriting. December 15
1980
  • One hundred of Sri Chinmoy’s books presented to Brown University, Rhode Island. February 17
  • Named a Visiting Lecturer at the University of Washington School of Art. May 8
  • Offered lecture, “The Inner Role of the United Nations,” at the US State Department’s “Open Forum.” June 6
  • Ripley’s Believe It or Not nationally-syndicated cartoon carried Sri Chinmoy’s The Goal is Won achievement. June 18
  • Special publication of I Know Not Why, 27 Bengali poems and translations written in Sri Chinmoy’s handwriting, in honor of his 49th birthday. August 27
  • Delivered his definitive speech on his running philosophy, “The Inner Running and the Outer Running”, University of Oregon. October 17
  • Offered his classic speech on gratitude, “I Thank You,” at the New York Ethical Culture Society. November 27
1981
  • Special publication of My 50 Gratitude-Summers, a hardbound, cloth-covered book of 50 aphorisms written in Sri Chinmoy’s handwriting in honor of his 50th birthday. August 27
1982
  • Publication of 500th book, I Am Ready, consisting of 40 aphorisms wriitten in Sri Chinmoy’s handwriting in honor of his father’s centenary. April 14
  • Offered “Rabindranath Tagore” lecture at the Tagore Celebration of the Vidya Bhavan and the Tagore Society, New York. May 8
  • Fifty of Sri Chinmoy’s books presented to the Yale University Divinity School Library. May 25
  • Forty-seven of Sri Chinmoy’s books presented to Columbia University Library. May 28
  • Special publication of Sri Chinmoy’s 513th book, I Meditate So That, consisting of 47 aphorisms written in Sri Chinmoy’s handwriting on the occasion of his 51st birthday. August 27
  • Invited to read his poetry at Joseph Papp’s Public Theatre, New York City, as part of the “Poets at the Public” series. October 11
  • Completed the 50th volume of his 100-volume Ten Thousand Flower-Flames series. November 27
  • Recited from memory one poem from each of the first fifty volumes of Ten Thousand Flower-Flames (as well as giving the poem number and identifying the cover photo of each volume). December 4
  • Special publication of My Heart’s Thirty-One Sacred Secrets, Part 1, 31 aphorisms written in Sri Chinmoy’s handwriting. December 9
  • Recited from memory My Heart’s Thirty-One Sacred Secrets, Part 1. December 9
  • “Beyond Within” column began in the NY Voice newspaper. December 12
    Sri Chinmoy’s poem “Immortality” published in Notable Poets of the Twentieth Century.
1983
  • Offered lecture, “Realisation,” at the Center for the Study of World Religions, Harvard Divinity School. April 19
  • A set of 300 of Sri Chinmoy’s books purchased by the New York Public Library. April 21
  • Completed his Ten Thousand Flower-Flames poetry series. July 3
    Began writing Twenty-Seven Thousand Aspiration-Plants poetry series. July 10
  • Sri Chinmoy’s play The Son, about the life of the Christ, performed at the United Nations. August 16
  • Began writing Silence Speaks, a series of daily meditations carried by bookstores in poster format. November 1
  • Completed his 600th book, I Pray So That. December 9
1984
  • The Kettledrum Theatre Company, London, performed Sri Chinmoy’s play The Son for the public in Manhattan, White Plains and Rhode Island. April
    The Sacred Fire play performed at the Dag Hammarskjöld Auditorium, United Nations. June 21
  • Two of Sri Chinmoy’s rhymed poems appeared in The Lyric, the oldest magazine in North America devoted to traditional poetry. Summer
    Completed 10,000th of his Twenty-Seven Thousand Aspiration-Plants series. December 10
1986
  • First USA lecture in Sri Chinmoy’s international lecture/concert/meditation series to many of the world’s major universities. Cornell University, NY. January 25.
  • Completed his 700th book, My God-Hunger Dreams. August 27
1987
  • Meeting with Mani Sankar Mukherji, leading author from Bangladesh, who subsequently wrote a series of Bengali articles on Sri Chinmoy. NY. October 5
1988
  • Presented a selection of his books to the Bharatiya Vidya Bhavan (USA) and The Hindu Temple Society of North America, at the conclusion his concert sponsored by these groups. July 23
  • Spoke on the Bhagavad Gita at the Geeta Temple, Queens, NY. November 13
1989
  • Began series of lectures and peace concerts at 25 major universities around the world, on the occasion of his silver jubilee in the West. Columbia University, NY. February 9
  • Sri Chinmoy’s Silver Jubilee book published, My Silver Jubilee Heart-Whispers. April 13
  • Named Honorary Patron of the Bharatiya Vidya Bhavan, India’s leading cultural association. April 21
  • Special birthday book published, The God of the Mind, 58 poems in his handwriting. August 15
  • Delivered talk from reviewing stand at India Day Parade, New York City. August 20
1990
  • Dr. P. Jayaraman, Executive Director of the Bharatiya Vidya Bhavan (USA), delivered the first in a series of several readings and interpretations of Sri Chinmoy’s poetry. The readings included Dr. Jayaraman’s translations of the poetry into Hindi. Buchman Hall, NY. April 11
  • Sri Chinmoy’s “Anniversary in the West” book published, God Is Kidnapped, a hardbound edition of 100 conversations between a seeker and his heart. April 13
  • Met with Felix Kuznetsov, Director of the Gorky Institute of World Literature of the Academy of Sciences in Moscow. Jamaica, NY. May 26
1991
  • The International Association of Educators for World Peace presented Sri Chinmoy with two diplomas of honor, for his work for peace: “Dharmaraja” and the “Fountain of Universal Peace” award. February 22
    The World Peace University presented Sri Chinmoy with its World Citizen Humanitarian Award. March 15
  • Completed his 800th book, Concentration, Meditation, Contemplation. April 16
  • Sri Chinmoy personally presented President Mikhail Gorbachev with his book of writings dedicated to the President, Gorbachev. The Master-Key of the Universal Heart. London, England. July 17

2006
  • Sri Chinmoy has now written over 1,500 books
